The Fédération Internationale de Football Association (FIFA) oversees the quadrennial competition of 32 men's national teams known as football. It selects the male world champion in the discipline. Each group plays a round-robin tournament, in which each team is scheduled for three matches against other teams in the same group. This means that a total of six matches are played within a group. The last round of matches of each group is scheduled at the same time to preserve fairness among all four teams.
